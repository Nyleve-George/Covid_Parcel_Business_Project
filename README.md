# Covid_Parcel_Business_Project
Analysis of the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on ABC Company’s parcel delivery business. This project uses weekly parcel shipment data from 2018–2020 to answer key business questions, including overall volume and revenue changes, customer behavior shifts, peak season performance, and the effect on different customer segments.

## COVID-19 Impact on ABC Company’s Parcel Business (Week 12–53)

### Overall Impact
  
Volume and revenue grew far above the normal growth rate (ISGR = 11.4%), confirming that COVID-19 led to a significant spike in parcel business. Active customers slightly declined, indicating minor churn.

---

### Initial COVID Impact
- Week 11, 2020 marked the first drastic divergence from 2019 volumes.  
- Compared to 2018–2019 trends, Week 11 indicates pandemic-driven changes.  
- Major events around this time:
  - Mar 11: WHO declared COVID-19 a pandemic  
  - Mar 16: School closures  
  - Mar 23: Emergency declarations  
- Visualization confirms that volume sharply increased after Week 11.

---

### Peak Season Analysis
- Peak season 2020 exceeded 2019 volumes.  
- Highlights pandemic-driven surge in holiday parcel deliveries.  
- Small customer segment experienced relative declines despite overall growth.
---

### Customer Segmentation & Growth

##### High-value customers (Enterprise/Large) grew the most; Small and Other segments were more volatile.
---

### Lost/New Customers
- Lost customers: Mostly in Small group (drop of 14 of Small customers and 1 medium).    
- Retention of Small customers could improve profitability.


### Volume & Revenue by Customer Group
- Enterprise & Large: Highest revenue contribution.  
- Medium & Small: Volume growth, but lower revenue.  
- Pandemic accelerated parcel volumes across all groups.

### Executive Takeaways
1. COVID-19 significantly boosted volume and revenue beyond normal growth expectations.  
2. Week 11, 2020 is the key inflection point and week 12 when the impacts begins to show.  
3. Peak season 2020 exceeded 2019 volumes, showing strong pandemic-driven e-commerce adoption.  
4. High-value customers drove most revenue growth; small/new accounts were more volatile.  
5. Retention strategies is needed for Small customers and growth focus on Medium segments would maximize revenue.  
6. Visualizations clearly show pandemic impact per customer group.
